Career Interests

It's no surprise that I'm interested in web development. I never expected that it'd be something I'd end up in, but seemingly every opportunity in my life has been pushing me more and more towards it, ha! Funny how things work out that way. But between my comp-sci teacher having me lead a club for two years, having a Co-op in the field, and now getting to work with this fantastic team, I've picked up more than a passing interest.
Aside from that, I've always had dreams of developing Operating Systems. I messed around with them a lot as a child, initially testing out a bunch on VMWare to test viruses that I found people testing on Youtube. My love for OS' really started when I got into Linux, however, and started spending hours tinkering with it and trying to tailor my computer exactly to my needs. I was just fascinated with low level the programming could get, and I feel like it's one of the fields where you *really* have to know the theory of everything on top of having the skill (something I value, given how my brain understands things). Of course, this could be a pipe dream. I can't say I've taken an operating systems class yet, and for all I know I might find it a complete bore!


Goal for this year

Over the past three or so years of my life, I've picked up a lot of scattered bits and pieces of web-dev knowledge. I've learnt a bit about mySQL, some PHP, some Node.JS, some html, some system administrations... many things, really, but I've never actually had a cohesive understanding of *all* of these at any given time. Every opportunity I got just happened to require I learn something new, and given that looming uni admissions meant I couldn't focus all my time into web development, I'd inevitably forget what I learnt previously. So I think my main goal this year is to finally get that cohesion I need to become a great web developer. I want to connect those many bits and pieces I have in my brain so that I can finally feel confident in my own skills.
